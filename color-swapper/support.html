Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why doesn't the app detect all colors in my design?

A: Color Swapper currently detects colors from text elements, shapes, and rectangles. Colors in images, gradients, or embedded elements are not detected as these require different APIs.

Q: Can I swap colors across all pages?

A: Currently, Color Swapper works on the current page only. You'll need to run it separately on each page of your design.

Q: I made a mistake. Can I undo the color changes?

A: Yes! Use Canva's undo feature (Ctrl+Z on Windows or Cmd+Z on Mac) immediately after applying changes to revert them.

Q: Why can't I see the "Apply changes" button?

A: The Apply button only appears after you've scanned for colors and set at least one color replacement. Click "Set color" next to a scanned color to choose a replacement.

Q: Why is the Apply button disabled?

A: The Apply button is disabled until you set at least one color mapping. Scan for colors first, then click "Set color" to choose replacement colors.

Q: Can I swap the same color to multiple different colors?

A: No, each source color can only be mapped to one target color. If you need different replacements, you would need to do multiple passes.

Q: Why don't I see any colors after scanning?

A: Make sure your design has elements that match your selected filter. If you selected "Text" but only have shapes, no colors will be found. Try using the "All" filter.

Q: Does this work with brand colors?

A: Yes! When you click "Set color", the Canva color picker opens which includes your brand colors. You can easily select from your brand palette.
