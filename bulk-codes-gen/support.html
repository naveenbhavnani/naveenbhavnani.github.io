Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What do the placeholders mean?

A: Placeholders define what characters appear in each position:

  • {A} - Uppercase letter (A-Z)
  • {a} - Lowercase letter (a-z)
  • {0} - Digit (0-9)
  • {X} - Uppercase letter or digit
  • {x} - Lowercase letter or digit
  • {L} - Any letter (upper or lower)
  • {*} - Any letter or digit

Q: What's the difference between Random and Sequential mode?

A: Random mode generates unique codes with random characters in each placeholder position. Sequential mode generates codes with incrementing numbers (only works with {0} placeholders).

Q: Why am I getting a "pattern capacity" error?

A: This means your pattern can't generate enough unique codes for the quantity you requested. For example, {0} can only generate 10 unique values (0-9). Add more placeholders or reduce the quantity.

Q: Are my generated codes saved?

A: No. Generated codes exist only in your current session. Once you close the app or generate new codes, previous codes are gone. Make sure to insert them into your design before closing.

Q: Can I generate more than 100 codes?

A: Currently, the maximum is 100 codes per batch. For more codes, you can generate multiple batches and combine them in your design.

Q: Why aren't my settings saved?

A: The app uses browser local storage to save your mode and pattern preferences. If you're using private/incognito mode or have cleared your browser data, settings won't persist.

Q: Can I guarantee codes are unique across multiple batches?

A: The app guarantees uniqueness within a single batch, but not across different generation sessions. For absolute uniqueness across batches, use sequential mode with different start values, or use longer random patterns with more placeholders.
